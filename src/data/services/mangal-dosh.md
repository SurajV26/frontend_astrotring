# What Is Manglik Dosh? The Astrological Foundation

In _Vedic astrology (Jyotish Shastra)_, Mars — known as **Mangal** or **Kuja** — is the planet of **energy, drive, ambition, courage, and aggression**.

It governs your **raw life force**, sexual energy, competitive spirit, and your capacity to take bold action.

Mars is also the **karaka (significator)** of:

- Brothers
- Siblings
- Friends
- Physical strength
- Real estate

**Manglik Dosh** (also spelled _Mangal Dosh_ or _Kuja Dosha_) occurs when Mars is placed in specific houses of the natal chart (_Janam Kundali_).

According to North Indian astrology traditions, if Mars sits in the **1st, 4th, 7th, 8th, or 12th house**, you are considered a **Manglik person**. In South Indian traditions, the **2nd house** is also included.

---

## Key Houses for Manglik Dosh (North Indian System)

- **1st House (Lagna / Ascendant)** – House of self, personality, and physical body
- **4th House** – Home, mother, comfort, domestic happiness
- **7th House** – Marriage, partnerships, spouse
- **8th House** – In-laws, longevity, transformation
- **12th House** – Foreign settlement, expenditure, moksha

Since Mars can occupy **5 out of 12 houses**, statistically around **43% of the world's population is Manglik**.

This debunks the myth that being Manglik is rare or uniquely unfortunate.

# Is Manglik Dosh Mentioned in Ancient Texts?

The concept of Manglik Dosh as widely practiced today is **not explicitly found in foundational Vedic astrology texts like Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra**.

The classification evolved later in regional traditions.

The historical fear likely arose due to high fertility risks in earlier times before modern medicine. With today’s medical advancements, that concern is largely obsolete.
