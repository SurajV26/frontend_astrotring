## What Is Ketu Dosh? Understanding the Planet of Detachment and Liberation

In **Vedic astrology (Jyotish Shastra)**, if Rahu is the planet of obsessive attachment — always wanting more, always grasping — then Ketu is its perfect opposite.

Where Rahu clings, Ketu releases.  
Where Rahu amplifies desire, Ketu whispers:

> “Let it go. You'll rebuild. Why hold on?”

Ketu is the only planet in the entire astrological system whose primary purpose is **liberation (Moksha)**.

It teaches detachment.  
It loosens your grip on the material world.

And in a civilization built around accumulation and attachment, that makes Ketu one of the most misunderstood — and most feared — planets in a birth chart.

When Ketu is poorly placed or creates challenges in the horoscope, the condition is known as **Ketu Dosh**.

---

# What Is Ketu? The Headless Planet of Intuition and Mysticism

In Vedic mythology:

- Rahu is the head
- Ketu is the tail

Ketu has **no eyes** — which means it cannot see the physical world.

Instead, it perceives:

- The unseen
- The spiritual
- The psychic
- The occult

Ketu is associated with:

- Intuition and psychic ability
- Mysticism and occult sciences
- Past-life karma
- Research and deep investigation
- Detachment and Moksha
- Confusion in material life

Ketu's headlessness is not weakness — it is higher perception.

# What Is Ketu Dosh? Definition and Formation

Ketu Dosh occurs when:

- Ketu occupies key life houses
- Ketu conjuncts malefics
- Ketu Mahadasha activates
- Ketu placed in uncomfortable signs

The core effect:

> Forced detachment from the house Ketu occupies.

This detachment is rarely voluntary.

# Ketu's Core Philosophy

The most important truth:

Wherever Ketu sits, you must learn to let go.

The moment you stop resisting detachment, Ketu begins to support you.

Example:

Ketu in 2nd house — money.

- Hoard money → struggle
- Treat money as energy → abundance

Alignment brings reward.
